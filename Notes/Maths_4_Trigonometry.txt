# SECTION 1: FUNDAMENTAL TRIGONOMETRIC RATIOS

## 1.1 Basic Definitions

In a right-angled triangle:
- **sin θ** = Opposite/Hypotenuse
- **cos θ** = Adjacent/Hypotenuse  
- **tan θ** = Opposite/Adjacent

**Reciprocal Ratios**:
- **cosec θ** = 1/sin θ = Hypotenuse/Opposite
- **sec θ** = 1/cos θ = Hypotenuse/Adjacent
- **cot θ** = 1/tan θ = Adjacent/Opposite

## 1.2 Signs of Trigonometric Functions in Quadrants

| Quadrant | sin | cos | tan | cosec | sec | cot |
|----------|-----|-----|-----|-------|-----|-----|
| I (0°-90°) | + | + | + | + | + | + |
| II (90°-180°) | + | - | - | + | - | - |
| III (180°-270°) | - | - | + | - | - | + |
| IV (270°-360°) | - | + | - | - | + | - |

**Memory Aid**: "All Students Take Coffee"
- **A**ll positive in Quadrant **A** (I)
- **S**in positive in Quadrant **S** (II)
- **T**an positive in Quadrant **T** (III)
- **C**os positive in Quadrant **C** (IV)

## 1.3 Standard Angle Values (MUST MEMORIZE)

| Angle (θ) | 0° | 30° | 45° | 60° | 90° | 180° | 270° | 360° |
|-----------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|------|------|------|
| sin θ | 0 | 1/2 | 1/√2 | √3/2 | 1 | 0 | -1 | 0 |
| cos θ | 1 | √3/2 | 1/√2 | 1/2 | 0 | -1 | 0 | 1 |
| tan θ | 0 | 1/√3 | 1 | √3 | ∞ | 0 | ∞ | 0 |
| cosec θ | ∞ | 2 | √2 | 2/√3 | 1 | ∞ | -1 | ∞ |
| sec θ | 1 | 2/√3 | √2 | 2 | ∞ | -1 | ∞ | 1 |
| cot θ | ∞ | √3 | 1 | 1/√3 | 0 | ∞ | 0 | ∞ |

**Memory Trick for sin values**: Write 0, 1, 2, 3, 4 → divide by 4 → take square root
- sin 0° = √(0/4) = 0
- sin 30° = √(1/4) = 1/2
- sin 45° = √(2/4) = 1/√2
- sin 60° = √(3/4) = √3/2
- sin 90° = √(4/4) = 1

cos values are reverse of sin values.

## 1.4 CAST Rule - Quick Quadrant Determination

**C**os positive (IV)
**A**ll positive (I)
**S**in positive (II)
**T**an positive (III)

Read anticlockwise: ASTC (All Sin Tan Cos)

## 1.5 Jog Your Memory - Section 1

| Concept | Formula/Trick |
|---------|---------------|
| Quadrant Signs | ASTC - All, Sin, Tan, Cos positive |
| Standard Values | √(0/4), √(1/4), √(2/4), √(3/4), √(4/4) for sin |
| Reciprocal | cosec = 1/sin, sec = 1/cos, cot = 1/tan |

# SECTION 2: TRIGONOMETRIC IDENTITIES

## 2.1 Pythagorean Identities

| Identity | Alternative Form |
|----------|------------------|
| sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 | sin²θ = 1 - cos²θ, cos²θ = 1 - sin²θ |
| 1 + tan²θ = sec²θ | tan²θ = sec²θ - 1, sec²θ - tan²θ = 1 |
| 1 + cot²θ = cosec²θ | cot²θ = cosec²θ - 1, cosec²θ - cot²θ = 1 |

## 2.2 Reciprocal Identities

| Identity | Alternative |
|----------|-------------|
| sin θ = 1/cosec θ | cosec θ = 1/sin θ |
| cos θ = 1/sec θ | sec θ = 1/cos θ |
| tan θ = 1/cot θ | cot θ = 1/tan θ |
| tan θ = sin θ/cos θ | cot θ = cos θ/sin θ |

## 2.3 Complementary Angle Identities (90° - θ)

| Function | Result |
|----------|--------|
| sin(90° - θ) | cos θ |
| cos(90° - θ) | sin θ |
| tan(90° - θ) | cot θ |
| cot(90° - θ) | tan θ |
| sec(90° - θ) | cosec θ |
| cosec(90° - θ) | sec θ |

**Memory Aid**: "Co-function pairs": sin↔cos, tan↔cot, sec↔cosec

## 2.4 Negative Angle Identities

| Identity | Result |
|----------|--------|
| sin(-θ) | -sin θ |
| cos(-θ) | cos θ |
| tan(-θ) | -tan θ |
| cosec(-θ) | -cosec θ |
| sec(-θ) | sec θ |
| cot(-θ) | -cot θ |

**Memory Aid**: Only cos and sec are even functions (remain positive), rest are odd.

## 2.5 Sum and Difference Formulas (CRITICAL)

| Formula | Expansion |
|---------|-----------|
| sin(A + B) | sin A cos B + cos A sin B |
| sin(A - B) | sin A cos B - cos A sin B |
| cos(A + B) | cos A cos B - sin A sin B |
| cos(A - B) | cos A cos B + sin A sin B |
| tan(A + B) | (tan A + tan B)/(1 - tan A tan B) |
| tan(A - B) | (tan A - tan B)/(1 + tan A tan B) |

**Memory Aid**: 
- Sine: "Same signs, mix functions" → sin(A+B) = sin cos + cos sin
- Cosine: "Opposite signs, same functions" → cos(A+B) = cos cos - sin sin

## 2.6 Double Angle Formulas

| Formula | Alternative Forms |
|---------|-------------------|
| sin 2A | 2 sin A cos A |
| cos 2A | cos²A - sin²A = 2 cos²A - 1 = 1 - 2 sin²A |
| tan 2A | 2 tan A/(1 - tan²A) |

**Important**: 
- sin 2A = 2 sin A cos A = 2 tan A/(1 + tan²A)
- cos 2A = (1 - tan²A)/(1 + tan²A)

## 2.7 Triple Angle Formulas

| Formula | Expansion |
|---------|-----------|
| sin 3A | 3 sin A - 4 sin³A |
| cos 3A | 4 cos³A - 3 cos A |
| tan 3A | (3 tan A - tan³A)/(1 - 3 tan²A) |

## 2.8 Half Angle Formulas

| Formula | Expression |
|---------|------------|
| sin(A/2) | ±√[(1 - cos A)/2] |
| cos(A/2) | ±√[(1 + cos A)/2] |
| tan(A/2) | ±√[(1 - cos A)/(1 + cos A)] = (1 - cos A)/sin A = sin A/(1 + cos A) |

**Sign** depends on quadrant of A/2.

## 2.9 Sum-to-Product Formulas

| Formula | Result |
|---------|--------|
| sin C + sin D | 2 sin[(C + D)/2] cos[(C - D)/2] |
| sin C - sin D | 2 cos[(C + D)/2] sin[(C - D)/2] |
| cos C + cos D | 2 cos[(C + D)/2] cos[(C - D)/2] |
| cos C - cos D | -2 sin[(C + D)/2] sin[(C - D)/2] |

**Memory Aid**: 
- Sum of sines → 2 sin(average) cos(half difference)
- Difference of sines → 2 cos(average) sin(half difference)

## 2.10 Product-to-Sum Formulas

| Formula | Result |
|---------|--------|
| 2 sin A cos B | sin(A + B) + sin(A - B) |
| 2 cos A sin B | sin(A + B) - sin(A - B) |
| 2 cos A cos B | cos(A + B) + cos(A - B) |
| 2 sin A sin B | cos(A - B) - cos(A + B) |

## 2.11 Special Product Formulas (Exam Specials)

**Product of Cosines Series**:
- cos θ × cos 2θ × cos 2²θ × ... × cos(2ⁿ⁻¹θ) = sin(2ⁿθ)/(2ⁿ sin θ), for θ ≠ nπ

**Example**: cos 20° × cos 40° × cos 80°
- Here, θ = 20°, n = 3
- = sin(2³ × 20°)/(2³ sin 20°) = sin 160°/(8 sin 20°)
- = sin(180° - 20°)/(8 sin 20°) = sin 20°/(8 sin 20°) = 1/8

**Sine and Cosine Series**:
- sin α + sin(α+β) + sin(α+2β) + ... + sin(α+(n-1)β) = [sin(nβ/2)/sin(β/2)] × sin(α + (n-1)β/2)
- cos α + cos(α+β) + cos(α+2β) + ... + cos(α+(n-1)β) = [sin(nβ/2)/sin(β/2)] × cos(α + (n-1)β/2)

## 2.12 Advanced Problem-Solving Strategies

### Strategy 1: Convert to Sine & Cosine
When you see sec, cosec, tan, cot - rewrite them in terms of sin and cos.

**Example**: Simplify (sec θ + tan θ)(1 - sin θ)

**Solution**:
= (1/cos θ + sin θ/cos θ)(1 - sin θ)
= ((1 + sin θ)/cos θ)(1 - sin θ)
= (1 - sin²θ)/cos θ
= cos²θ/cos θ = cos θ ✓

### Strategy 2: The "1" Substitution Trick
Replace 1 with sin²θ + cos²θ, sec²θ - tan²θ, or cosec²θ - cot²θ.

**Example**: Prove √[(1 - cos θ)/(1 + cos θ)] = cosec θ - cot θ

**Solution**:
Multiply numerator and denominator inside root by (1 - cos θ):
LHS = √[((1 - cos θ)²)/(1 - cos²θ)] = √[((1 - cos θ)²)/sin²θ]
= (1 - cos θ)/sin θ = 1/sin θ - cos θ/sin θ = cosec θ - cot θ ✓

### Strategy 3: Matching with Options
In MCQ, simplify until it matches one of the given options.

## 2.13 Conditional Identities

When A + B + C = π (or 180°):

| Identity | Formula |
|----------|---------|
| sin(A + B) | sin C |
| cos(A + B) | -cos C |
| tan(A + B) | -tan C |
| sin 2A + sin 2B + sin 2C | 4 sin A sin B sin C |
| cos 2A + cos 2B + cos 2C | -1 - 4 cos A cos B cos C |
| sin A + sin B + sin C | 4 cos(A/2) cos(B/2) cos(C/2) |
| cos A + cos B + cos C | 1 + 4 sin(A/2) sin(B/2) sin(C/2) |
| tan A + tan B + tan C | tan A tan B tan C |

## 2.14 Solved Examples with Shortcuts

**Example 1**: If sin θ + cos θ = √2, find tan θ + cot θ.

**Shortcut**:
- Square both sides: sin²θ + cos²θ + 2 sin θ cos θ = 2
- 1 + 2 sin θ cos θ = 2
- sin θ cos θ = 1/2
- tan θ + cot θ = (sin θ/cos θ) + (cos θ/sin θ) = (sin²θ + cos²θ)/(sin θ cos θ) = 1/(1/2) = 2

**Example 2**: If tan A = 1/2 and tan B = 1/3, find A + B.

**Shortcut**:
- tan(A + B) = (1/2 + 1/3)/(1 - 1/2 × 1/3) = (5/6)/(5/6) = 1
- ∴ A + B = 45° or π/4

**Example 3**: Prove that (1 + tan θ tan 2θ) = sec 2θ.

**Solution**:
LHS = 1 + (sin θ/cos θ) × (sin 2θ/cos 2θ)
= 1 + [sin θ × 2 sin θ cos θ]/(cos θ × cos 2θ)
= 1 + (2 sin²θ)/cos 2θ
= 1 + [2(1 - cos²θ)]/cos 2θ
= 1 + [2 - (1 + cos 2θ)]/cos 2θ [since cos²θ = (1+cos 2θ)/2]
= 1 + [1 - cos 2θ]/cos 2θ
= 1 + sec 2θ - 1 = sec 2θ ✓

## 2.15 Jog Your Memory - Section 2

| Category | Key Formulas |
|----------|---------------|
| Pythagorean | sin²θ + cos²θ = 1, 1 + tan²θ = sec²θ, 1 + cot²θ = cosec²θ |
| Sum/Difference | sin(A±B) = sinA cosB ± cosA sinB, cos(A±B) = cosA cosB ∓ sinA sinB |
| Double Angle | sin2A = 2 sinA cosA, cos2A = 2cos²A - 1 = 1 - 2sin²A |
| Product Series | cosθ cos2θ ... cos(2ⁿ⁻¹θ) = sin(2ⁿθ)/(2ⁿ sinθ) |
| Conditional (A+B+C=π) | tanA + tanB + tanC = tanA tanB tanC |

# SECTION 3: TRIGONOMETRIC EQUATIONS

## 3.1 General Solutions

| Equation | General Solution | Principal Values |
|----------|------------------|-------------------|
| sin θ = 0 | θ = nπ, n ∈ Z | θ = 0, π, 2π,... |
| cos θ = 0 | θ = (2n + 1)π/2, n ∈ Z | θ = π/2, 3π/2,... |
| tan θ = 0 | θ = nπ, n ∈ Z | θ = 0, π, 2π,... |

| Equation | General Solution |
|----------|------------------|
| sin θ = sin α | θ = nπ + (-1)ⁿα, n ∈ Z |
| cos θ = cos α | θ = 2nπ ± α, n ∈ Z |
| tan θ = tan α | θ = nπ + α, n ∈ Z |
| sin²θ = sin²α | θ = nπ ± α, n ∈ Z |
| cos²θ = cos²α | θ = nπ ± α, n ∈ Z |
| tan²θ = tan²α | θ = nπ ± α, n ∈ Z |

## 3.2 Important Particular Cases

| Equation | Solution |
|----------|----------|
| sin θ = 1 | θ = (4n + 1)π/2 |
| sin θ = -1 | θ = (4n - 1)π/2 |
| cos θ = 1 | θ = 2nπ |
| cos θ = -1 | θ = (2n + 1)π |
| tan θ = 1 | θ = nπ + π/4 |
| tan θ = -1 | θ = nπ - π/4 |

## 3.3 Principal Value in Given Interval - Shortcut

To find solutions in [0, 2π] or [0°, 360°]:

**Step 1**: Find reference angle α (positive acute angle)
**Step 2**: Use ASTC rule to identify quadrants where function has given sign
**Step 3**: Calculate angles:
- Quadrant I: α
- Quadrant II: π - α (180° - α)
- Quadrant III: π + α (180° + α)
- Quadrant IV: 2π - α (360° - α)

**Example**: Solve sin θ = -1/2 in [0, 2π]

**Solution**:
- Reference angle α = 30° (π/6) [since sin 30° = 1/2]
- sin is negative in QIII and QIV
- QIII: θ = π + π/6 = 7π/6 (210°)
- QIV: θ = 2π - π/6 = 11π/6 (330°)

## 3.4 Solving by Bounding Technique

Since -1 ≤ sin θ ≤ 1 and -1 ≤ cos θ ≤ 1:

**Example**: Solve sin θ + cos θ = 2

**Shortcut**: Maximum value of sin θ and cos θ individually is 1. Their sum can be 2 only if both are simultaneously 1, i.e., sin θ = 1 AND cos θ = 1. No such θ exists. ∴ No solution.

**Example**: Solve 3 sin θ + 4 cos θ = 7

**Shortcut**: Maximum value of 3 sin θ + 4 cos θ = √(3² + 4²) = 5. Since 7 > 5, no solution.

## 3.5 Equations of Form a cos θ + b sin θ = c

**Method**:
1. Divide by √(a² + b²)
2. Let cos φ = a/√(a²+b²), sin φ = b/√(a²+b²)
3. Equation becomes cos(θ - φ) = c/√(a²+b²) or sin(θ + φ) = c/√(a²+b²)
4. Condition for solution: |c| ≤ √(a² + b²)

**Example**: Solve √3 cos θ + sin θ = 1

**Solution**:
- √(a²+b²) = √(3+1) = 2
- Divide: (√3/2) cos θ + (1/2) sin θ = 1/2
- cos 30° cos θ + sin 30° sin θ = 1/2
- cos(θ - 30°) = 1/2 = cos 60°
- θ - 30° = ±60° + 360°n
- θ = 90° + 360°n or θ = -30° + 360°n
- Principal values: θ = 90°, 330°

## 3.6 Quadratic Equations in Trigonometric Functions

**Example**: Solve 2 cos²θ + 3 sin θ = 0

**Solution**:
- 2(1 - sin²θ) + 3 sin θ = 0
- 2 - 2 sin²θ + 3 sin θ = 0
- 2 sin²θ - 3 sin θ - 2 = 0
- Let x = sin θ: 2x² - 3x - 2 = 0
- (2x + 1)(x - 2) = 0
- x = -1/2 or x = 2 (impossible)
- sin θ = -1/2
- θ = 210°, 330° (in 0° to 360°)

## 3.7 Equations Involving Multiple Angles

**Example**: Solve tan 3θ = 1

**Solution**:
- tan 3θ = 1 = tan π/4
- 3θ = nπ + π/4
- θ = nπ/3 + π/12, n ∈ Z

## 3.8 Equations Using Factorization

**Example**: Solve sin 2θ = cos θ

**Solution**:
- 2 sin θ cos θ - cos θ = 0
- cos θ (2 sin θ - 1) = 0
- Either cos θ = 0 → θ = (2n+1)π/2
- Or sin θ = 1/2 → θ = nπ + (-1)ⁿ(π/6)

## 3.9 Solved Examples with Tricks

**Example 1**: Solve sin²θ - sin θ - 2 = 0

**Trick**: 
- Let x = sin θ
- x² - x - 2 = 0
- (x - 2)(x + 1) = 0
- x = 2 (reject) or x = -1
- sin θ = -1 → θ = (4n - 1)π/2

**Example 2**: Solve tan θ + cot θ = 2

**Trick**:
- tan θ + 1/tan θ = 2
- Let t = tan θ
- t + 1/t = 2
- t² - 2t + 1 = 0
- (t - 1)² = 0 → t = 1
- tan θ = 1 → θ = nπ + π/4

**Example 3**: Solve sin 7θ + sin 4θ + sin θ = 0

**Trick**: Use sum-to-product
- (sin 7θ + sin θ) + sin 4θ = 0
- 2 sin 4θ cos 3θ + sin 4θ = 0
- sin 4θ (2 cos 3θ + 1) = 0
- Either sin 4θ = 0 → 4θ = nπ → θ = nπ/4
- Or cos 3θ = -1/2 → 3θ = 2nπ ± 2π/3 → θ = 2nπ/3 ± 2π/9

## 3.10 Jog Your Memory - Section 3

| Equation Type | Solution Method |
|---------------|-----------------|
| sin θ = sin α | θ = nπ + (-1)ⁿα |
| cos θ = cos α | θ = 2nπ ± α |
| tan θ = tan α | θ = nπ + α |
| a cos θ + b sin θ = c | Convert to cos(θ-φ) = c/√(a²+b²) |
| Quadratic in sin/cos | Substitute variable, solve, then find θ |

# SECTION 4: INVERSE TRIGONOMETRIC FUNCTIONS

## 4.1 Definitions and Principal Value Branches

| Function | Domain | Range (Principal Value) | Quadrant |
|----------|--------|------------------------|----------|
| sin⁻¹x | [-1, 1] | [-π/2, π/2] | IV & I |
| cos⁻¹x | [-1, 1] | [0, π] | I & II |
| tan⁻¹x | R | (-π/2, π/2) | IV & I |
| cot⁻¹x | R | (0, π) | I & II |
| sec⁻¹x | (-∞, -1] ∪ [1, ∞) | [0, π/2) ∪ (π/2, π] | I & II |
| cosec⁻¹x | (-∞, -1] ∪ [1, ∞) | [-π/2, 0) ∪ (0, π/2] | IV & I |

**Memory Aid for Ranges**:
- sin⁻¹, tan⁻¹, cosec⁻¹ → Right side angles (QIV and QI)
- cos⁻¹, cot⁻¹, sec⁻¹ → Upper side angles (QI and QII)

## 4.2 Important Properties

### Property 1: Complementary Relations
- sin⁻¹x + cos⁻¹x = π/2
- tan⁻¹x + cot⁻¹x = π/2
- sec⁻¹x + cosec⁻¹x = π/2

### Property 2: Negative Arguments
- sin⁻¹(-x) = -sin⁻¹x
- cos⁻¹(-x) = π - cos⁻¹x
- tan⁻¹(-x) = -tan⁻¹x
- cot⁻¹(-x) = π - cot⁻¹x
- sec⁻¹(-x) = π - sec⁻¹x
- cosec⁻¹(-x) = -cosec⁻¹x

### Property 3: Reciprocal Relations
- sin⁻¹(1/x) = cosec⁻¹x, for |x| ≥ 1
- cos⁻¹(1/x) = sec⁻¹x, for |x| ≥ 1
- tan⁻¹(1/x) = cot⁻¹x, for x > 0
- tan⁻¹(1/x) = -π + cot⁻¹x, for x < 0

### Property 4: Sum/Difference Formulas

**For tan⁻¹**:
- tan⁻¹x + tan⁻¹y = tan⁻¹[(x + y)/(1 - xy)], if xy < 1
- tan⁻¹x + tan⁻¹y = π + tan⁻¹[(x + y)/(1 - xy)], if x > 0, y > 0, xy > 1
- tan⁻¹x + tan⁻¹y = -π + tan⁻¹[(x + y)/(1 - xy)], if x < 0, y < 0, xy > 1
- tan⁻¹x - tan⁻¹y = tan⁻¹[(x - y)/(1 + xy)]

**For sin⁻¹**:
- sin⁻¹x + sin⁻¹y = sin⁻¹[x√(1-y²) + y√(1-x²)]
- sin⁻¹x - sin⁻¹y = sin⁻¹[x√(1-y²) - y√(1-x²)]

**For cos⁻¹**:
- cos⁻¹x + cos⁻¹y = cos⁻¹[xy - √(1-x²)√(1-y²)]
- cos⁻¹x - cos⁻¹y = cos⁻¹[xy + √(1-x²)√(1-y²)]

## 4.3 Function of Inverse Formulas (CRITICAL)

These are direct applications:

| Formula | Result |
|---------|--------|
| sin(cos⁻¹x) | √(1 - x²) |
| cos(sin⁻¹x) | √(1 - x²) |
| tan(sin⁻¹x) | x/√(1 - x²) |
| tan(cos⁻¹x) | √(1 - x²)/x |
| sin(tan⁻¹x) | x/√(1 + x²) |
| cos(tan⁻¹x) | 1/√(1 + x²) |

## 4.4 Simplification using Substitution

**Example**: Simplify sin(cot⁻¹x)

**Method**: 
- Let θ = cot⁻¹x → cot θ = x
- Construct right triangle: adjacent = x, opposite = 1, hypotenuse = √(1 + x²)
- sin θ = opposite/hypotenuse = 1/√(1 + x²)
- ∴ sin(cot⁻¹x) = 1/√(1 + x²)

## 4.5 Solved Examples

**Example 1**: Find value of sin⁻¹(1/2) + cos⁻¹(1/2)

**Shortcut**: Using complementary property: sin⁻¹x + cos⁻¹x = π/2
∴ Answer = π/2

**Example 2**: Prove tan⁻¹(1/2) + tan⁻¹(1/3) = π/4

**Solution**:
- tan⁻¹(1/2) + tan⁻¹(1/3) = tan⁻¹[(1/2 + 1/3)/(1 - 1/2 × 1/3)]
- = tan⁻¹[(5/6)/(5/6)] = tan⁻¹(1) = π/4

**Example 3**: Solve tan⁻¹(2x) + tan⁻¹(3x) = π/4

**Solution**:
- tan⁻¹[(2x + 3x)/(1 - 6x²)] = π/4
- 5x/(1 - 6x²) = 1
- 5x = 1 - 6x²
- 6x² + 5x - 1 = 0
- (x + 1)(6x - 1) = 0
- x = -1 or x = 1/6
- Check condition: For x = -1, xy = 3 > 1, formula gives π + ..., not valid
- ∴ x = 1/6

**Example 4**: Evaluate sin(cos⁻¹(3/5))

**Shortcut**: sin(cos⁻¹x) = √(1 - x²) = √(1 - 9/25) = √(16/25) = 4/5

**Example 5**: Evaluate tan[½ sin⁻¹(2x/(1 + x²)) + ½ cos⁻¹((1 - y²)/(1 + y²))]

**Recall**:
- sin⁻¹(2x/(1 + x²)) = 2 tan⁻¹x for |x| ≤ 1
- cos⁻¹((1 - y²)/(1 + y²)) = 2 tan⁻¹y for y ≥ 0

**Solution**:
= tan[½ × 2 tan⁻¹x + ½ × 2 tan⁻¹y]
= tan[tan⁻¹x + tan⁻¹y]
= tan[tan⁻¹((x + y)/(1 - xy))] = (x + y)/(1 - xy)

**Example 6**: Find value of tan⁻¹(1) + tan⁻¹(2) + tan⁻¹(3)

**Trick**: Note that 1 + 2 + 3 = 6 and 1×2×3 = 6
- For A+B+C = π, tan A + tan B + tan C = tan A tan B tan C
- Here, if tan A = 1, tan B = 2, tan C = 3, then tan A + tan B + tan C = 6 = tan A tan B tan C
- ∴ A + B + C = π
- So tan⁻¹(1) + tan⁻¹(2) + tan⁻¹(3) = π

## 4.6 Jog Your Memory - Section 4

| Property | Formula |
|----------|---------|
| Complementary | sin⁻¹x + cos⁻¹x = π/2 |
| Negative | sin⁻¹(-x) = -sin⁻¹x, cos⁻¹(-x) = π - cos⁻¹x |
| tan⁻¹ sum | tan⁻¹x + tan⁻¹y = tan⁻¹[(x+y)/(1-xy)], xy<1 |
| Function of inverse | sin(cos⁻¹x) = √(1-x²), tan(sin⁻¹x) = x/√(1-x²) |
| Triple tan identity | If tan⁻¹a + tan⁻¹b + tan⁻¹c = π, then a+b+c = abc |

# SECTION 5: PROPERTIES OF TRIANGLES

## 5.1 Notation

For triangle ABC:
- **Angles**: A, B, C (in degrees or radians)
- **Sides opposite to angles**: a, b, c respectively
- **Perimeter**: 2s = a + b + c (s = semi-perimeter)
- **Area**: Δ
- **Circumradius**: R
- **Inradius**: r
- **Exradii**: r₁, r₂, r₃ (opposite to vertices A, B, C respectively)

## 5.2 Sine Rule (Fundamental)

**Formula**: a/sin A = b/sin B = c/sin C = 2R

**Alternative**: a : b : c = sin A : sin B : sin C

**Applications**:
- Two angles and one side (AAS or ASA)
- Two sides and a non-included angle (SSA)

**Example**: In ΔABC, if A = 60°, B = 45°, a = 10, find b.
- b = (a × sin B)/sin A = (10 × sin 45°)/sin 60° = (10 × 1/√2)/(√3/2) = 20/√6

## 5.3 Cosine Rule

**Formulas**:
- a² = b² + c² - 2bc cos A
- b² = a² + c² - 2ac cos B
- c² = a² + b² - 2ab cos C

**Alternative Forms**:
- cos A = (b² + c² - a²)/(2bc)
- cos B = (a² + c² - b²)/(2ac)
- cos C = (a² + b² - c²)/(2ab)

**Applications**:
- Three sides (SSS)
- Two sides and included angle (SAS)

**Example**: In ΔABC, a = 8, b = 6, c = 4, find cos A.
- cos A = (b² + c² - a²)/(2bc) = (36 + 16 - 64)/(2 × 6 × 4) = (-12)/(48) = -1/4

## 5.4 Projection Rule

**Formulas**:
- a = b cos C + c cos B
- b = c cos A + a cos C
- c = a cos B + b cos A

## 5.5 Napier's Analogies (Tangent Rule)

**Formulas**:
- tan[(B - C)/2] = [(b - c)/(b + c)] cot(A/2)
- tan[(C - A)/2] = [(c - a)/(c + a)] cot(B/2)
- tan[(A - B)/2] = [(a - b)/(a + b)] cot(C/2)

**Application**: When two sides and included angle are given, to find other angles quickly.

**Example**: In ΔABC, a = 18, b = 12, C = 60°. Find A and B.
- A + B = 120°
- tan[(A - B)/2] = (18-12)/(18+12) × cot(30°) = (6/30) × √3 = √3/5
- (A - B)/2 = tan⁻¹(√3/5)
- Then A = [(A+B) + (A-B)]/2, B = [(A+B) - (A-B)]/2

## 5.6 Half-Angle Formulas

**Sine half-angle**:
- sin(A/2) = √[(s - b)(s - c)/(bc)]
- sin(B/2) = √[(s - c)(s - a)/(ca)]
- sin(C/2) = √[(s - a)(s - b)/(ab)]

**Cosine half-angle**:
- cos(A/2) = √[s(s - a)/(bc)]
- cos(B/2) = √[s(s - b)/(ca)]
- cos(C/2) = √[s(s - c)/(ab)]

**Tangent half-angle**:
- tan(A/2) = √[(s - b)(s - c)/{s(s - a)}] = Δ/[s(s - a)]
- tan(B/2) = Δ/[s(s - b)]
- tan(C/2) = Δ/[s(s - c)]

## 5.7 Area Formulas (Multiple Forms)

1. **Using two sides and included angle**:
   - Δ = ½ bc sin A = ½ ca sin B = ½ ab sin C

2. **Heron's Formula**:
   - Δ = √[s(s - a)(s - b)(s - c)]

3. **Using circumradius**:
   - Δ = abc/(4R)

4. **Using inradius**:
   - Δ = rs

5. **Using sides and angles**:
   - Δ = (b² sin C sin A)/(2 sin B) (and similar cyclic forms)

## 5.8 Circumradius (R)

**Formulas**:
- R = a/(2 sin A) = b/(2 sin B) = c/(2 sin C)
- R = abc/(4Δ)

**Special case**: In right triangle with hypotenuse c, R = c/2

## 5.9 Inradius (r)

**Formulas**:
- r = Δ/s
- r = (a sin(B/2) sin(C/2))/cos(A/2)
- r = 4R sin(A/2) sin(B/2) sin(C/2)

## 5.10 Exradii (r₁, r₂, r₃)

**Formulas**:
- r₁ = Δ/(s - a) [excircle opposite A]
- r₂ = Δ/(s - b) [excircle opposite B]
- r₃ = Δ/(s - c) [excircle opposite C]

**Relations**:
- 1/r = 1/r₁ + 1/r₂ + 1/r₃
- r₁ + r₂ + r₃ - r = 4R

## 5.11 m-n Theorem (Important for Cevians)

If a cevian from vertex C divides side AB in ratio m:n (i.e., AD:DB = m:n) and makes angle δ with AB, then:

**(m + n) cot δ = m cot α - n cot β**

or alternatively: (m + n) cot δ = n cot B - m cot A

## 5.12 Length of Angle Bisector

Length of bisector of angle A (from A to side a):
- **Formula**: lₐ = (2bc cos(A/2))/(b + c)
- **Alternative**: lₐ² = bc[1 - (a²/(b + c)²)]

## 5.13 Length of Median

Length of median from A to side a:
- **Formula**: mₐ = ½√(2b² + 2c² - a²)

## 5.14 Orthocenter and Distances

- Distance of orthocenter from vertex A = 2R cos A
- Distance of orthocenter from side BC = 2R cos B cos C

## 5.15 Solved Examples

**Example 1**: In ΔABC, a = 13, b = 14, c = 15. Find area, R, r.

**Solution**:
- s = (13 + 14 + 15)/2 = 21
- Δ = √[21(21-13)(21-14)(21-15)] = √[21 × 8 × 7 × 6] = √7056 = 84
- R = abc/(4Δ) = (13 × 14 × 15)/(4 × 84) = 2730/336 = 8.125
- r = Δ/s = 84/21 = 4

**Example 2**: In ΔABC, if a = 18, b = 24, c = 30, find R and r.

**Shortcut**: Check if right triangle: 18² + 24² = 324 + 576 = 900 = 30², so C = 90°
- Δ = ½ × 18 × 24 = 216
- R = c/2 = 15 (hypotenuse is diameter)
- s = (18 + 24 + 30)/2 = 36
- r = Δ/s = 216/36 = 6

**Example 3**: Prove that in any triangle, a(b cos C - c cos B) = b² - c²

**Solution**:
Using cosine rule:
- b cos C = (a² + b² - c²)/(2a)
- c cos B = (a² + c² - b²)/(2a)
- LHS = a[(a² + b² - c²)/(2a) - (a² + c² - b²)/(2a)]
- = (a² + b² - c² - a² - c² + b²)/2
- = (2b² - 2c²)/2 = b² - c² = RHS

**Example 4**: In ΔABC, if A = 60°, b = 10, c = 12, find a.

**Solution**:
- a² = b² + c² - 2bc cos A
- a² = 100 + 144 - 2 × 10 × 12 × 1/2
- a² = 244 - 120 = 124
- a = 2√31

# SECTION 6: SOLUTION OF TRIANGLES

## 6.1 Types of Triangle Problems

| Case | Given | Use | Condition |
|------|-------|-----|-----------|
| 1 | Three sides (SSS) | Cosine rule to find angles | Triangle inequality |
| 2 | Two sides and included angle (SAS) | Cosine rule for third side, then sine rule | - |
| 3 | Two angles and one side (ASA or AAS) | Sine rule | A+B+C = 180° |
| 4 | Two sides and non-included angle (SSA) | Sine rule | Ambiguous case |

## 6.2 Step-by-Step Solution Methods

### Case 1: SSS (Three sides given)
1. Use cosine rule to find largest angle first (opposite largest side)
2. Use sine rule or cosine rule for second angle
3. Third angle = 180° - sum of first two

### Case 2: SAS (Two sides and included angle)
1. Use cosine rule to find third side
2. Use sine rule to find smaller angle (opposite smaller side)
3. Third angle = 180° - sum of first two

### Case 3: ASA/AAS (Two angles and one side)
1. Find third angle using A + B + C = 180°
2. Use sine rule to find remaining sides

### Case 4: SSA (Two sides and non-included angle) - Ambiguous Case

## 6.3 Ambiguous Case (SSA) - Detailed Analysis

Given: sides a, b and angle A (opposite side a)

**Step 1**: Calculate h = b sin A (height from C)

**Step 2**: Apply decision tree:

```
                    ┌─────────────────┐
                    │   Given a, b, A  │
                    │   h = b sin A    │
                    └─────────┬────────┘
                              │
            ┌─────────────────┼─────────────────┐
            │                 │                 │
            ▼                 ▼                 ▼
        a < h            a = h            a > h
           │                 │                 │
           ▼                 ▼                 │
    ┌──────────────┐  ┌──────────────┐        │
    │ No Triangle  │  │ One Triangle │        │
    │              │  │ (Right-angled│        │
    └──────────────┘  │ at B)        │        │
                      └──────────────┘        │
                                              │
                            ┌─────────────────┼─────────────────┐
                            │                 │                 │
                            ▼                 ▼                 ▼
                        a < b             a = b             a > b
                           │                 │                 │
                           ▼                 ▼                 ▼
                    ┌──────────────┐  ┌──────────────┐  ┌──────────────┐
                    │ Two Triangles│  │ One Triangle │  │ One Triangle │
                    │ B acute/obtuse│  │ (Isosceles)  │  │ (B acute)    │
                    └──────────────┘  └──────────────┘  └──────────────┘
```

**Step 3**: Calculate sin B = (b sin A)/a
- If sin B > 1: No triangle
- If sin B = 1: One right triangle (B = 90°)
- If sin B < 1:
  - If a ≥ b: One triangle (B acute)
  - If a < b: Two triangles (B₁ acute, B₂ obtuse)

## 6.4 Solved Examples - All Cases

### Example 1: SSS Case
Solve ΔABC if a = 7, b = 8, c = 9

**Solution**:
- Largest side is c = 9, so find angle C first
- cos C = (a² + b² - c²)/(2ab) = (49 + 64 - 81)/(2 × 7 × 8) = 32/112 = 2/7
- C = cos⁻¹(2/7) ≈ 73.4°
- Using sine rule: sin A = (a sin C)/c = (7 × sin 73.4°)/9 ≈ (7 × 0.958)/9 ≈ 0.745
- A = sin⁻¹(0.745) ≈ 48.2° (acute since a < c)
- B = 180° - A - C = 180° - 48.2° - 73.4° = 58.4°

### Example 2: SAS Case
Solve ΔABC if b = 12, c = 10, A = 60°

**Solution**:
- a² = b² + c² - 2bc cos A = 144 + 100 - 2 × 12 × 10 × 0.5 = 244 - 120 = 124
- a = √124 ≈ 11.14
- Using sine rule: sin B = (b sin A)/a = (12 × 0.866)/11.14 ≈ 10.392/11.14 ≈ 0.933
- B = sin⁻¹(0.933) ≈ 68.9° or 111.1°
- Check: Since a < b? Actually 11.14 < 12, so a < b → possible two triangles
- For B₁ = 68.9°: C₁ = 180° - 60° - 68.9° = 51.1°
- For B₂ = 111.1°: C₂ = 180° - 60° - 111.1° = 8.9° (valid)
- ∴ Two possible triangles

### Example 3: ASA Case
Solve ΔABC if A = 50°, B = 60°, a = 10

**Solution**:
- C = 180° - 50° - 60° = 70°
- Using sine rule: b = (a sin B)/sin A = (10 × sin 60°)/sin 50° = (10 × 0.866)/0.766 ≈ 11.31
- c = (a sin C)/sin A = (10 × sin 70°)/sin 50° = (10 × 0.94)/0.766 ≈ 12.27

### Example 4: SSA Case - No Triangle
Solve ΔABC if a = 5, b = 8, A = 30°

**Solution**:
- h = b sin A = 8 × 0.5 = 4
- a = 5 > h? Yes, 5 > 4, so proceed
- sin B = (b sin A)/a = (8 × 0.5)/5 = 4/5 = 0.8
- B = sin⁻¹(0.8) ≈ 53.1° or 126.9°
- Check a < b? 5 < 8, so both possible?
- For B₁ = 53.1°: C₁ = 180° - 30° - 53.1° = 96.9° (valid)
- For B₂ = 126.9°: C₂ = 180° - 30° - 126.9° = 23.1° (valid)
- ∴ Two triangles possible

**Correction**: Actually both are valid, so two triangles exist.

### Example 5: SSA Case - One Triangle
Solve ΔABC if a = 10, b = 8, A = 50°

**Solution**:
- h = b sin A = 8 × 0.766 = 6.128
- a = 10 > h, proceed
- sin B = (b sin A)/a = (8 × 0.766)/10 = 6.128/10 = 0.6128
- B = sin⁻¹(0.6128) ≈ 37.8° or 142.2°
- Check a < b? 10 > 8, so a > b → only acute B possible
- B = 37.8°, C = 180° - 50° - 37.8° = 92.2°
- Only one triangle

# SECTION 7: HEIGHTS AND DISTANCES

## 7.1 Basic Terminology

- **Angle of Elevation**: Angle made by line of sight with horizontal when object is ABOVE observer
- **Angle of Depression**: Angle made by line of sight with horizontal when object is BELOW observer
- **Line of Sight**: Straight line from observer's eye to object

## 7.2 Key Relationships

In right triangle: 
- tan θ = height/distance (opposite/adjacent)
- sin θ = height/hypotenuse
- cos θ = distance/hypotenuse

## 7.3 Standard Right Triangle Ratios

| Angle | sin | cos | tan | Side Ratio |
|-------|-----|-----|-----|------------|
| 30° | 1/2 | √3/2 | 1/√3 | 1 : √3 : 2 |
| 45° | 1/√2 | 1/√2 | 1 | 1 : 1 : √2 |
| 60° | √3/2 | 1/2 | √3 | √3 : 1 : 2 |

## 7.4 Common Problem Types

### Type 1: Single Object - Single Observer

**Example**: A tower stands vertically. From a point 100 m away, angle of elevation is 30°. Find height.

**Solution**:
- tan 30° = h/100
- h = 100 × tan 30° = 100/√3 ≈ 57.74 m

### Type 2: Two Objects - Single Observer

**Example**: From the top of a building 60 m high, angles of depression of top and bottom of a lamp post are 30° and 60°. Find lamp post height.

**Solution**:
- Let building height = 60 m, lamp post height = h m, distance = x
- For bottom: tan 60° = 60/x → x = 60/√3 = 20√3 m
- For top: tan 30° = (60 - h)/x
- 1/√3 = (60 - h)/(20√3)
- 20 = 60 - h → h = 40 m

### Type 3: Single Object - Two Observers (Same Side)

**Example**: Two points on ground 100 m apart, angles of elevation of tower are 30° and 45°. Find tower height.

**Solution**:
- Let height = h, distance from first point = x
- tan 45° = h/x → x = h
- tan 30° = h/(x + 100)
- 1/√3 = h/(h + 100)
- h + 100 = h√3
- 100 = h(√3 - 1)
- h = 100/(√3 - 1) = 100(√3 + 1)/2 = 50(√3 + 1) m

### Type 4: Single Object - Two Observers (Opposite Sides)

**Example**: From a point on bridge across river, angles of depression of banks on opposite sides are 30° and 45°. Bridge height = 6 m. Find river width.

**Solution**:
- Let distances from foot to banks = x and y
- tan 30° = 6/x → x = 6√3 m
- tan 45° = 6/y → y = 6 m
- Width = x + y = 6(√3 + 1) m

### Type 5: Cloud/Aeroplane Problems

**Example**: Angle of elevation of cloud from point 20 m above lake is 30°, angle of depression of its reflection is 60°. Find cloud height above lake.

**Solution**:
- Let cloud height above lake = h m, horizontal distance = x
- For cloud: tan 30° = (h - 20)/x → 1/√3 = (h - 20)/x ...(1)
- For reflection (appears at depth h below surface):
- Total vertical distance = 20 + h
- tan 60° = (20 + h)/x → √3 = (20 + h)/x ...(2)
- From (1): x = √3(h - 20)
- From (2): x = (20 + h)/√3
- Equating: √3(h - 20) = (20 + h)/√3
- 3(h - 20) = 20 + h
- 3h - 60 = 20 + h
- 2h = 80 → h = 40 m

### Type 6: Speed/Distance Problems

**Example**: An aeroplane flying horizontally at height of 3000 m observes angles of depression of two points on opposite banks of river as 45° and 30°. If points are in line with plane, find width of river and speed if time to cross is 30 seconds.

**Solution**:
- Let plane at height 3000 m
- For first point: tan 45° = 3000/x₁ → x₁ = 3000 m
- For second point: tan 30° = 3000/x₂ → x₂ = 3000√3 m
- Width = |x₂ - x₁| = 3000(√3 - 1) m
- Speed = distance/time = 3000(√3 - 1)/30 = 100(√3 - 1) m/s

### Type 7: Pillar/Building with Shadow

**Example**: A pillar casts a shadow of length 20 m when sun's altitude is 60°. Find length of shadow when sun's altitude is 30°.

**Solution**:
- Let pillar height = h
- At 60°: tan 60° = h/20 → h = 20√3 m
- At 30°: tan 30° = h/x → 1/√3 = 20√3/x
- x = 20√3 × √3 = 60 m

## 7.5 Shortcuts and Tricks

### Trick 1: 30-60-90 Triangle Ratios
In 30-60-90 triangle, sides are in ratio 1 : √3 : 2
- Side opposite 30° = (1/2) × hypotenuse
- Side opposite 60° = (√3/2) × hypotenuse

### Trick 2: 45-45-90 Triangle Ratios
In 45-45-90 triangle, sides are in ratio 1 : 1 : √2
- Both legs equal
- Hypotenuse = leg × √2

### Trick 3: Common Values Approximation
- √2 ≈ 1.414
- √3 ≈ 1.732
- 1/√2 ≈ 0.707
- 1/√3 ≈ 0.577

### Trick 4: For Two-Observer Problems
If angles are complementary (e.g., 30° and 60°):
- h² = d₁ × d₂ (where d₁, d₂ are distances from foot)

# SECTION 9: SHORTCUTS AND TRICKS COMPILATION

## 9.1 Identity Shortcuts

1. **Convert to sin/cos** when multiple functions appear
2. **Use 1 = sin²θ + cos²θ** to simplify expressions
3. **For product series** cos θ cos 2θ ... cos(2ⁿ⁻¹θ) = sin(2ⁿθ)/(2ⁿ sin θ)
4. **For A+B+C = π**: tan A + tan B + tan C = tan A tan B tan C

## 9.2 Equation Shortcuts

1. **sin θ + cos θ = √2 sin(θ + 45°)** = √2 cos(θ - 45°)
2. **Maximum value** of a sin θ + b cos θ = √(a² + b²)
3. **Minimum value** = -√(a² + b²)
4. **For sin θ + cos θ = k**, square to find sin 2θ

## 9.3 Inverse Shortcuts

1. **sin⁻¹x + cos⁻¹x = π/2** always
2. **tan⁻¹x + tan⁻¹y = tan⁻¹[(x+y)/(1-xy)]** when xy < 1
3. **sin(cos⁻¹x) = √(1-x²)** (memorize these)
4. **tan(sin⁻¹x) = x/√(1-x²)**

## 9.4 Triangle Shortcuts

1. **Check right triangle** using a² + b² = c²
2. **For right triangle**: R = hypotenuse/2, r = (a+b-c)/2
3. **Area** using Heron's formula when three sides given
4. **Napier's analogy** for quick angle difference
5. **Ambiguous case**: Compare a with h = b sin A and with b

## 9.5 Height-Distance Shortcuts

1. **30-60-90 triangle**: sides 1 : √3 : 2
2. **45-45-90 triangle**: sides 1 : 1 : √2
3. **For complementary angles** (30° & 60°): h² = d₁ × d₂
4. **Approximate values**: √3 ≈ 1.732, √2 ≈ 1.414

**Q1.** If sin θ + cos θ = √2, then tan θ + cot θ = ?
a) 1
b) 2
c) √2
d) 0

**Solution**:
- Square: sin²θ + cos²θ + 2 sin θ cos θ = 2
- 1 + 2 sin θ cos θ = 2
- sin θ cos θ = 1/2
- tan θ + cot θ = (sin²θ + cos²θ)/(sin θ cos θ) = 1/(1/2) = 2
**Answer: b) 2**

**Q2.** The value of cos 20° cos 40° cos 80° is:
a) 1/4
b) 1/8
c) 1/2
d) 1

**Solution**:
- Using product formula: cos θ cos 2θ cos 4θ = sin 8θ/(8 sin θ)
- Here θ = 20°, so = sin 160°/(8 sin 20°)
- sin 160° = sin(180° - 20°) = sin 20°
- = sin 20°/(8 sin 20°) = 1/8
**Answer: b) 1/8**

**Q3.** If tan⁻¹(1/2) + tan⁻¹(1/3) = θ, then θ = ?
a) 30°
b) 45°
c) 60°
d) 90°

**Solution**:
- tan⁻¹(1/2) + tan⁻¹(1/3) = tan⁻¹[(1/2+1/3)/(1-1/6)]
- = tan⁻¹[(5/6)/(5/6)] = tan⁻¹(1) = 45°
**Answer: b) 45°**

**Q4.** In ΔABC, if a = 13, b = 14, c = 15, then R = ?
a) 65/8
b) 65/4
c) 65/2
d) 65

**Solution**:
- s = (13+14+15)/2 = 21
- Δ = √[21×8×7×6] = √7056 = 84
- R = abc/(4Δ) = (13×14×15)/(4×84) = 2730/336 = 65/8
**Answer: a) 65/8**

**Q5.** The general solution of sin θ = 1/2 is:
a) θ = nπ + (-1)ⁿ(π/6)
b) θ = 2nπ ± π/6
c) θ = nπ + π/6
d) θ = nπ ± π/6

**Answer: a) θ = nπ + (-1)ⁿ(π/6)**

**Q6.** From the top of a building 60 m high, the angles of depression of the top and bottom of a lamp post are 30° and 60°. The height of lamp post is:
a) 20 m
b) 30 m
c) 40 m
d) 50 m

**Solution**:
- For bottom: tan 60° = 60/x → x = 20√3
- For top: tan 30° = (60-h)/x
- 1/√3 = (60-h)/(20√3) → 20 = 60-h → h = 40 m
**Answer: c) 40 m**

**Q7.** In ΔABC, if a = 8, b = 6, c = 4, then cos A = ?
a) 1/4
b) -1/4
c) 1/2
d) -1/2

**Solution**:
- cos A = (b² + c² - a²)/(2bc) = (36 + 16 - 64)/(2×6×4) = (-12)/48 = -1/4
**Answer: b) -1/4**

**Q8.** The value of sin(cos⁻¹(4/5)) is:
a) 3/5
b) 4/5
c) 3/4
d) 4/3

**Solution**:
- sin(cos⁻¹x) = √(1-x²) = √(1-16/25) = √(9/25) = 3/5
**Answer: a) 3/5**

**Q9.** If tan θ + cot θ = 2, then sin θ = ?
a) 0
b) 1
c) ±1
d) ±1/√2

**Solution**:
- tan θ + 1/tan θ = 2
- tan²θ - 2 tan θ + 1 = 0
- (tan θ - 1)² = 0 → tan θ = 1
- θ = 45° → sin θ = 1/√2
**Answer: d) ±1/√2**

**Q10.** A tower casts a shadow of length 30 m when sun's altitude is 60°. The length of shadow when sun's altitude is 30° is:
a) 60 m
b) 90 m
c) 30√3 m
d) 30 m

**Solution**:
- At 60°: tan 60° = h/30 → h = 30√3
- At 30°: tan 30° = h/x → 1/√3 = 30√3/x → x = 90 m
**Answer: b) 90 m**

# SECTION 11: LAST MINUTE REVISION - ONE PAGE SUMMARY

## 11.1 MUST-KNOW FORMULAS

### Identities
- sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
- 1 + tan²θ = sec²θ
- 1 + cot²θ = cosec²θ

### Sum/Difference
- sin(A+B) = sinA cosB + cosA sinB
- cos(A+B) = cosA cosB - sinA sinB
- tan(A+B) = (tanA + tanB)/(1 - tanA tanB)

### Double Angle
- sin2A = 2 sinA cosA
- cos2A = 2cos²A - 1 = 1 - 2sin²A

### Inverse
- sin⁻¹x + cos⁻¹x = π/2
- tan⁻¹x + tan⁻¹y = tan⁻¹[(x+y)/(1-xy)], xy<1
- sin(cos⁻¹x) = √(1-x²)

### Triangle Properties
- Sine Rule: a/sinA = b/sinB = c/sinC = 2R
- Cosine Rule: cosA = (b²+c²-a²)/(2bc)
- Area: Δ = ½ bc sinA = √[s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)] = rs
- Napier's Analogy: tan[(A-B)/2] = (a-b)/(a+b) cot(C/2)

### Equations
- sinθ = sinα → θ = nπ + (-1)ⁿα
- cosθ = cosα → θ = 2nπ ± α
- tanθ = tanα → θ = nπ + α
