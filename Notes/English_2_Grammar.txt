# SECTION 1: ERROR DETECTION (SPOTTING ERRORS)

## 📌 What is Error Detection?
A sentence is divided into **3-4 parts** (labeled A, B, C, D). You must identify which part contains the grammatical mistake. If no error exists, choose "No error" (usually option D).

**Example Format:**
```
The team of players (A) / are preparing (B) / for the match. (C) / No error (D)
```

## 🎯 **SHORTCUTS & TRICKS FOR ERROR DETECTION**

### ⚡ **The 10-Second Scan Method**

When you see an error detection question, quickly scan for these in order:

| Step | What to Check | Time |
|------|---------------|------|
| 1 | Subject-Verb Agreement | 2 sec |
| 2 | Tense (look for time words) | 2 sec |
| 3 | Prepositions after verbs | 1 sec |
| 4 | Articles (a/an/the) | 1 sec |
| 5 | Pronouns (who/whom, I/me) | 1 sec |
| 6 | Parallelism in lists | 1 sec |
| 7 | Redundancy | 1 sec |
| 8 | Comparatives | 1 sec |

### ⚡ **Trick: The "Sound Test"**
Read the sentence in your mind. If it sounds wrong, it probably IS wrong. Trust your ear for 80% of questions!

### ⚡ **Trick: Elimination Technique**
1. Identify parts that are DEFINITELY correct
2. Focus on the remaining 1-2 suspicious parts
3. Apply grammar rules to the suspicious part
4. If all parts seem correct, choose "No error"

### ⚡ **Trick: Common Error Probability**
In MAH MCA CET, errors are most likely in:
- **Part B** (40% probability)
- **Part C** (30% probability)
- **Part A** (20% probability)
- **No error** (10% probability)

## 📋 **DETAILED ERROR TYPES WITH EXAMPLES**

### TYPE 1: SUBJECT-VERB AGREEMENT ERRORS

#### Rule 1: Singular Subject → Singular Verb
| Incorrect | Correct | Explanation |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| She **go** to school. | She **goes** to school. | She (singular) needs goes |
| He **play** cricket. | He **plays** cricket. | He (singular) needs plays |
| The boy **run** fast. | The boy **runs** fast. | Boy (singular) needs runs |

#### Rule 2: Plural Subject → Plural Verb
| Incorrect | Correct | Explanation |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| They **goes** to school. | They **go** to school. | They (plural) needs go |
| The boys **runs** fast. | The boys **run** fast. | Boys (plural) needs run |

#### Rule 3: Each/Every/Either/Neither + Noun → Singular Verb
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| Each student **are** ready. | Each student **is** ready. |
| Every boy **have** passed. | Every boy **has** passed. |
| Either option **are** fine. | Either option **is** fine. |
| Neither answer **were** correct. | Neither answer **was** correct. |

#### Rule 4: Neither...Nor / Either...Or → Verb agrees with NEAREST subject
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| Neither Ram nor his friends **is** coming. | Neither Ram nor his friends **are** coming. |
| Neither his friends nor Ram **are** coming. | Neither his friends nor Ram **is** coming. |
| Either the teacher or the students **is** wrong. | Either the teacher or the students **are** wrong. |
| Either the students or the teacher **are** wrong. | Either the students or the teacher **is** wrong. |

#### 🔥 **SHORTCUT:** In Neither/Nor and Either/Or, look at the SECOND subject (the one after nor/or). That decides the verb!

#### Rule 5: One of + Plural Noun → Singular Verb
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| One of the boys **are** absent. | One of the boys **is** absent. |
| One of the cars **were** damaged. | One of the cars **was** damaged. |
| One of the best students **have** won. | One of the best students **has** won. |

#### 🔥 **SHORTCUT:** "One of" ALWAYS takes singular verb. Remember: One → Singular

#### Rule 6: The number of → Singular | A number of → Plural
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| The number of students **are** increasing. | The number of students **is** increasing. |
| A number of students **is** present. | A number of students **are** present. |

#### 🔥 **SHORTCUT:** 
- "The number" (The = definite = singular)
- "A number" (A = indefinite = many = plural)

#### Rule 7: Collective Nouns (Team, Family, Crowd, Committee) → Usually Singular
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| The team **are** playing well. | The team **is** playing well. |
| The family **have** arrived. | The family **has** arrived. |
| The committee **were** unanimous. | The committee **was** unanimous. |

**Exception:** If members act individually → Plural
- The family **are** eating dinner. (each person eating separately)
- The team **are** arguing among themselves. (individual members arguing)

#### Rule 8: Nouns ending in 's' but Singular (News, Mathematics, Physics)
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| The news **are** good. | The news **is** good. |
| Mathematics **are** difficult. | Mathematics **is** difficult. |
| Economics **were** his favorite. | Economics **was** his favorite. |

**List of such words:**
- News, Mathematics, Physics, Economics, Politics, Athletics, Gymnastics, Measles, Mumps, Rickets

#### Rule 9: Nouns always Plural (Scissors, Trousers, Glasses)
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| This scissors **is** sharp. | These scissors **are** sharp. |
| His trousers **was** torn. | His trousers **were** torn. |
| The glasses **is** on the table. | The glasses **are** on the table. |

#### Rule 10: Measurements/Amounts → Usually Singular
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| Ten kilometers **are** a long distance. | Ten kilometers **is** a long distance. |
| Five thousand rupees **are** too much. | Five thousand rupees **is** too much. |
| Two hours **are** enough time. | Two hours **is** enough time. |

#### Rule 11: There/Here sentences → Verb agrees with subject AFTER verb
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| There **is** many books. | There **are** many books. |
| Here **come** the bus. | Here **comes** the bus. |

#### Rule 12: Titles/Names → Singular even if plural in form
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| "The Adventures of Tom Sawyer" **are** famous. | "The Adventures of Tom Sawyer" **is** famous. |
| The United States **are** powerful. | The United States **is** powerful. |

#### Rule 13: Relative Pronouns (who/which/that) → Verb agrees with ANTECEDENT
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| He is one of those who **works** hard. | He is one of those who **work** hard. |
| This is the boy who **work** hard. | This is the boy who **works** hard. |

#### 🔥 **SHORTCUT:** Find what "who/which/that" refers to. If it refers to plural → plural verb, singular → singular verb.

#### Rule 14: Subjects with "as well as, along with, together with, including"
→ Verb agrees with FIRST subject

| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| The teacher, along with the students, **are** going. | The teacher, along with the students, **is** going. |
| Ram, as well as his friends, **were** present. | Ram, as well as his friends, **was** present. |

#### 🔥 **SHORTCUT:** Ignore the phrases between commas (as well as, along with, together with). Look at the first subject only!

### TYPE 2: TENSE ERRORS

#### Rule 1: Past Time Marker → Past Tense
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| I have seen him **yesterday**. | I **saw** him yesterday. |
| She has gone **last week**. | She **went** last week. |
| They have arrived **ago**. | They **arrived** ago. |

**Time Markers for Past Tense:**
- yesterday, last night/week/month/year, ago, in 1990, the other day

#### Rule 2: Since/For → Present Perfect or Perfect Continuous
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| She is living here **since** 2020. | She **has been living** here since 2020. |
| I know him **for** 5 years. | I **have known** him for 5 years. |

**Time Markers for Present Perfect:**
- since, for, already, just, ever, never, yet, recently, lately

#### Rule 3: Sequence of Tenses (Indirect Speech)
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| He said that he **is** coming. | He said that he **was** coming. |
| She told me that she **will** help. | She told me that she **would** help. |

**Rule:** If reporting verb is in past tense (said, told), the reported speech must be in past tense.

#### Rule 4: Habitual Actions → Present Tense
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| He **is going** to school daily. | He **goes** to school daily. |
| She **is waking** up at 6 AM every day. | She **wakes** up at 6 AM every day. |

#### Rule 5: Universal Truths → Present Tense (even in past context)
| Correct Usage |
|---------------|
| The teacher said that the earth **is** round. (NOT was) |
| He knew that water **boils** at 100°C. (NOT boiled) |

#### 📊 **TENSE TIME MARKERS QUICK REFERENCE**

| Tense | Time Markers |
|-------|--------------|
| Simple Present | always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never, every day |
| Present Continuous | now, at present, at the moment, Look! Listen! |
| Present Perfect | since, for, already, just, ever, never, yet, recently |
| Simple Past | yesterday, last night, ago, in 2000, the other day |
| Past Continuous | at 5 PM yesterday, while, when (interrupted action) |
| Past Perfect | before, after, already, by the time |
| Future | tomorrow, next week, soon, in the future |
| Future Perfect | by tomorrow, by next week, by 2025 |

### TYPE 4: ARTICLE ERRORS (A/AN/THE)

#### Rule 1: 'A' before consonant SOUND (not letter)
| Incorrect | Correct | Reason |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| **an** university | **a** university | "university" starts with "yu" sound (consonant) |
| **an** European | **a** European | "European" starts with "yu" sound |
| **an** one-eyed man | **a** one-eyed man | "one" starts with "wu" sound |
| **an** useful thing | **a** useful thing | "useful" starts with "yu" sound |
| **a** honest man | **an** honest man | "honest" starts with "o" sound (vowel) |
| **a** hour | **an** hour | "hour" starts with "ow" sound (vowel) |
| **a** MCA student | **an** MCA student | "MCA" starts with "em" sound (vowel) |
| **a** FBI agent | **an** FBI agent | "FBI" starts with "ef" sound (vowel) |

#### 🔥 **SHORTCUT FOR A/AN:**
- Say the word aloud. If the FIRST SOUND is a vowel (a, e, i, o, u) → use "an"
- If the FIRST SOUND is a consonant → use "a"

**Sound Check Examples:**
- University = "Yoo-niversity" (Y = consonant) → a university
- Honest = "O-nest" (O = vowel) → an honest
- Hour = "Ow-er" (Ow = vowel) → an hour
- One = "Wun" (W = consonant) → a one-eyed
- European = "Yoo-ro-pean" (Y = consonant) → a European
- MCA = "Em-see-ay" (E = vowel) → an MCA

#### Rule 2: First mention → a/an, Subsequent mention → the
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| I saw **a** lion. **A** lion was big. | I saw **a** lion. **The** lion was big. |

#### Rule 3: Unique things → 'the'
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| Sun is bright. | **The** sun is bright. |
| Moon is beautiful. | **The** moon is beautiful. |
| Earth is round. | **The** earth is round. |
| Sky is blue. | **The** sky is blue. |

#### Rule 4: Superlatives → 'the'
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| He is **best** student. | He is **the** best student. |
| This is **most** expensive. | This is **the** most expensive. |
| She is **tallest** girl. | She is **the** tallest girl. |

#### Rule 5: Ordinals (first, second, last) → 'the'
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| He came **first**. | He came **the** first. |
| This is **second** time. | This is **the** second time. |
| She was **last** to arrive. | She was **the** last to arrive. |

#### Rule 6: Musical instruments → 'the'
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| She plays **piano**. | She plays **the** piano. |
| He learns **guitar**. | He learns **the** guitar. |

#### Rule 7: Before rivers, seas, oceans, mountain ranges → 'the'
| Correct Usage |
|---------------|
| **the** Ganges, **the** Yamuna |
| **the** Pacific Ocean, **the** Arabian Sea |
| **the** Himalayas, **the** Alps |

#### Rule 8: Before countries with plural names or Republic/Kingdom → 'the'
| Correct Usage |
|---------------|
| **the** United States, **the** United Kingdom |
| **the** Netherlands, **the** Philippines |
| **the** Czech Republic |

#### Rule 9: Before adjectives representing a class → 'the'
| Correct Usage |
|---------------|
| **the** rich, **the** poor, **the** young, **the** elderly |
| **the** disabled, **the** unemployed |

#### 📌 **WHEN NOT TO USE 'THE'**

| Situation | Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Proper nouns (names) | **the** Mumbai | Mumbai |
| Abstract nouns (general) | **the** life is hard | Life is hard |
| Plural nouns (general) | **the** dogs are loyal | Dogs are loyal |
| Meals (general) | **the** breakfast is ready | Breakfast is ready |
| Languages | **the** Hindi is easy | Hindi is easy |
| Games/Sports | **the** cricket is fun | Cricket is fun |
| Most countries | **the** India | India |
| Streets/Roads | **the** Park Street | Park Street |
| Mountains (single) | **the** Mount Everest | Mount Everest |
| Lakes | **the** Lake Superior | Lake Superior |

### TYPE 5: PRONOUN ERRORS

#### 📌 **PRONOUN CASES**

| Subject Pronoun | Object Pronoun | Possessive Adjective | Possessive Pronoun | Reflexive |
|-----------------|----------------|----------------------|-------------------|-----------|
| I | me | my | mine | myself |
| You | you | your | yours | yourself |
| He | him | his | his | himself |
| She | her | her | hers | herself |
| It | it | its | its | itself |
| We | us | our | ours | ourselves |
| They | them | their | theirs | themselves |

#### Rule 1: Subject Pronoun (I, he, she, they, we) → Used as SUBJECT of verb
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| **Me** and my friend went. | **My friend and I** went. |
| **Him** and her are coming. | **He** and **she** are coming. |
| **Us** students are ready. | **We** students are ready. |

#### 🔥 **SHORTCUT:** If the pronoun is DOING the action → use subject pronoun (I, he, she, they, we)

#### Rule 2: Object Pronoun (me, him, her, them, us) → Used as OBJECT of verb/preposition
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| Between you and **I** | Between you and **me** |
| She gave it to **he** and **I** | She gave it to **him** and **me** |
| Please call **she** and **I** | Please call **her** and **me** |

#### 🔥 **SHORTCUT:** If the pronoun is RECEIVING the action or comes after a preposition → use object pronoun (me, him, her, them, us)

#### Rule 3: Who vs Whom
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| The man **whom** is here | The man **who** is here |
| The man **who** I saw | The man **whom** I saw |

#### 🔥 **THE WHO/WHOM TRICK:**
- If you can replace with **HE/SHE/THEY** → use **WHO**
- If you can replace with **HIM/HER/THEM** → use **WHOM**

**Examples:**
- "The man ___ is here" → **HE** is here → WHO
- "The man ___ I saw" → I saw **HIM** → WHOM
- "___ did you meet?" → You met **HIM** → WHOM
- "___ is coming?" → **HE** is coming → WHO

#### Rule 4: Each/Everyone/Everybody/Someone/Anybody/No one → Singular Pronouns
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| Everyone brought **their** book. | Everyone brought **his/her** book. |
| Each student must submit **their** assignment. | Each student must submit **his/her** assignment. |
| Nobody likes **their** boss. | Nobody likes **his/her** boss. |

#### 🔥 **SHORTCUT:** Words ending in -one, -body, -thing are SINGULAR → use singular pronouns

#### Rule 5: Reflexive Pronouns (myself, yourself, himself)
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| He did it **hisself**. | He did it **himself**. |
| They enjoyed **theirselves**. | They enjoyed **themselves**. |
| Please give it to **myself**. | Please give it to **me**. |

**When to use Reflexive Pronouns:**
1. Subject and object are same: I hurt **myself**.
2. For emphasis: I **myself** saw it.

#### Rule 6: Possessive before Gerund (-ing form as noun)
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| I hate **him** coming late. | I hate **his** coming late. |
| Do you mind **me** smoking? | Do you mind **my** smoking? |

#### Rule 7: Its vs It's
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| The dog wagged **it's** tail. | The dog wagged **its** tail. |
| **Its** a beautiful day. | **It's** a beautiful day. |

**Difference:**
- **Its** = possessive form of it
- **It's** = it is (contraction)

#### Rule 8: Your vs You're
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| **Your** late for class. | **You're** late for class. |
| I like **you're** dress. | I like **your** dress. |

**Difference:**
- **Your** = possessive form of you
- **You're** = you are (contraction)

#### Rule 9: Their vs There vs They're
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| **There** car is red. | **Their** car is red. |
| **Their** is a problem. | **There** is a problem. |
| **There** coming tomorrow. | **They're** coming tomorrow. |

**Difference:**
- **Their** = possessive form of they
- **There** = place or existence
- **They're** = they are (contraction)

### TYPE 6: ADJECTIVE VS ADVERB ERRORS

#### Rule 1: Adjective describes Noun | Adverb describes Verb
| Incorrect | Correct | Explanation |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| She is **beautifully**. | She is **beautiful**. | Describes noun "she" → adjective |
| She sings **beautiful**. | She sings **beautifully**. | Describes verb "sings" → adverb |
| He is **careful** driver. | He is a **careful** driver. | Describes noun "driver" → adjective |
| He drives **careful**. | He drives **carefully**. | Describes verb "drives" → adverb |

#### Rule 2: Linking Verbs (be, seem, appear, look, feel, smell, taste, sound, become) → Take Adjectives
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| She feels **badly**. | She feels **bad**. |
| It tastes **sweetly**. | It tastes **sweet**. |
| He looks **angrily**. | He looks **angry**. |
| The soup smells **deliciously**. | The soup smells **delicious**. |

#### 🔥 **SHORTCUT:** After linking verbs (is, am, are, was, were, seem, appear, look, feel, smell, taste, sound, become) → use ADJECTIVE, not adverb

#### Rule 3: Good vs Well
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| She sings **good**. | She sings **well**. |
| I am **well** at math. | I am **good** at math. |
| He is a **well** player. | He is a **good** player. |

**Difference:**
- **Good** = adjective (describes nouns)
- **Well** = adverb (describes verbs) OR adjective meaning "healthy"

**Examples:**
- She is a **good** singer. (adjective describing singer)
- She sings **well**. (adverb describing sings)
- I am **well**. (adjective meaning healthy)

#### Rule 4: Real vs Really
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| It is **real** beautiful. | It is **really** beautiful. |
| She is **real** intelligent. | She is **really** intelligent. |

**Difference:**
- **Real** = adjective (describes nouns)
- **Really** = adverb (describes adjectives/verbs)

#### Rule 5: Sure vs Surely
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| He **sure** is smart. | He **surely** is smart. |
| I am **surely** about it. | I am **sure** about it. |

### TYPE 7: PARALLELISM ERRORS

#### Rule: Items in a list must have the SAME GRAMMATICAL FORM

| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| She likes **dancing**, **to sing**, and **reading**. | She likes **dancing**, **singing**, and **reading**. |
| He is **intelligent**, **hardworking**, and **has honesty**. | He is **intelligent**, **hardworking**, and **honest**. |
| I came **to see**, **to learn**, and **understanding**. | I came **to see**, **to learn**, and **to understand**. |
| The exam was **long**, **difficult**, and **frustratingly**. | The exam was **long**, **difficult**, and **frustrating**. |

#### 🔥 **PARALLELISM PATTERNS:**

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Noun, noun, and noun | She bought apples, bananas, and oranges. |
| Adjective, adjective, and adjective | He is tall, dark, and handsome. |
| -ing, -ing, and -ing | She enjoys reading, writing, and painting. |
| to + verb, to + verb, and to + verb | He wants to eat, to sleep, and to relax. |
| Verb, verb, and verb | They ran, jumped, and shouted. |

#### Common Parallelism Errors in Correlative Conjunctions:

| Correlative Pair | Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------------|-----------|---------|
| not only...but also | He **not only** lost his ticket **but also** his luggage. | He lost **not only** his ticket **but also** his luggage. |
| either...or | You can **either** have tea **or** coffee. | ✓ (if followed by same form) |
| neither...nor | He **neither** eats fish **nor** meat. | He eats **neither** fish **nor** meat. |
| both...and | She is **both** intelligent **and** hardworking. | ✓ |

### TYPE 8: DOUBLE NEGATIVE ERRORS

#### Rule: Only ONE negative per sentence

| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| I don't know **nothing**. | I don't know **anything**. |
| He didn't see **nobody**. | He didn't see **anybody**. |
| She never goes **nowhere**. | She never goes **anywhere**. |
| I can't find it **nowhere**. | I can't find it **anywhere**. |
| He hasn't got **no** money. | He hasn't got **any** money. |
| We didn't do **nothing**. | We didn't do **anything**. |

#### 📌 **NEGATIVE WORDS:**
- no, not, never, nothing, nobody, nowhere, none, neither
- hardly, scarcely, barely (these are also negative)

#### 🔥 **SHORTCUT:** If you see two negatives in a sentence, one of them is wrong. Replace the second negative with "any" word.

**Negative + Any Rule:**
- nothing → anything
- nobody → anybody
- nowhere → anywhere
- none → any
- no → any

### TYPE 9: COMPARATIVE/SUPERLATIVE ERRORS

#### Rule 1: No Double Comparatives
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| She is **more smarter** than me. | She is **smarter** than me. |
| This is **more better**. | This is **better**. |
| He is **most tallest**. | He is **tallest**. |

#### Rule 2: Absolute Adjectives (cannot be compared)
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| This is **more unique**. | This is **unique**. |
| It is **most perfect**. | It is **perfect**. |
| She is **more dead** than alive. | She is dead. |

**List of Absolute Adjectives:**
- unique, perfect, complete, entire, total, absolute
- impossible, eternal, infinite, universal
- round, square, circular, triangular
- dead, alive, pregnant, married

#### Rule 3: Two items → Comparative (better) | Three+ items → Superlative (best)
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| He is the **best** of the two. | He is the **better** of the two. |
| She is the **taller** of the three. | She is the **tallest** of the three. |
| This is the **better** option among all. | This is the **best** option among all. |

#### Rule 4: Comparative with "than" | Superlative with "of/in"
| Correct Usage |
|---------------|
| She is **taller than** me. |
| She is the **tallest of** all. |
| She is the **tallest in** the class. |

#### Rule 5: Avoid self-comparison with "any"
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| She is taller than **any** student. | She is taller than **any other** student. |
| Mumbai is bigger than **any** city. | Mumbai is bigger than **any other** city. |

**Reason:** Without "other", she is comparing herself to herself (she is also a student)

#### Rule 6: Comparative Forms

| Adjective | Comparative | Superlative |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| good | better | best |
| bad | worse | worst |
| far | farther (distance) / further (additional) | farthest / furthest |
| little | less | least |
| many/much | more | most |

### TYPE 10: CONJUNCTION ERRORS

#### Rule 1: Though/Although + clause → No "but"
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| **Though** he tried **but** he failed. | **Though** he tried, he failed. |
| **Although** she was tired **but** she worked. | **Although** she was tired, she worked. |

#### Rule 2: Because/Since/As + clause → No "so"
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| **Because** he was ill **so** he stayed home. | **Because** he was ill, he stayed home. |
| **Since** it was raining **so** we stayed in. | **Since** it was raining, we stayed in. |

#### Rule 3: Correlative Conjunctions must be paired correctly

| Pair | Incorrect | Correct |
|------|-----------|---------|
| neither...nor | He **neither** eats fish **or** meat. | He **neither** eats fish **nor** meat. |
| either...or | He **either** eats fish **nor** meat. | He **either** eats fish **or** meat. |
| not only...but also | He **not only** eats fish **but** meat. | He **not only** eats fish **but also** meat. |
| both...and | He **both** eats fish **and** meat. | ✓ |
| whether...or | I don't know **whether** he comes **or** not. | ✓ |

#### Rule 4: No sooner...than | Hardly/Scarcely...when
| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| No sooner did he see the police **when** he ran. | No sooner did he see the police **than** he ran. |
| Hardly had he reached **than** it started. | Hardly had he reached **when** it started. |

#### 🔥 **SHORTCUT:**
- No sooner → than
- Hardly/Scarcely → when/before

### TYPE 11: MISPLACED MODIFIERS

#### Rule: The modifier should be NEXT to what it describes

| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| **Running fast**, the bus was missed by him. | **Running fast**, he missed the bus. |
| **Covered in rust**, he bought the old car. | He bought the old car **that was covered in rust**. |
| I saw a dog **on the way to school**. | **On the way to school**, I saw a dog. |
| She almost drove her kids to school every day. | She drove her kids to school **almost every day**. |
| He sold the car to the man **with leather seats**. | He sold the car **with leather seats** to the man. |

#### 🔥 **DANGLING MODIFIER TRICK:**
If a sentence starts with an -ing phrase, the next word MUST be the doer of that action.

❌ **Walking down the street**, the building looked beautiful. (Building can't walk)
✔ **Walking down the street**, I saw a beautiful building. (I can walk)

❌ **To pass the exam**, hard work is needed. (Hard work can't pass exam)
✔ **To pass the exam**, you need to work hard.

| **Join together** | Join |

### TYPE 13: CONDITIONAL SENTENCE ERRORS

#### 📊 **CONDITIONAL TYPES**

| Type | Usage | Structure | Example |
|------|-------|-----------|---------|
| Zero | Always true | If + present, present | If you heat ice, it melts. |
| First | Possible/Real | If + present, will + V1 | If it rains, we will cancel. |
| Second | Unlikely/Imaginary | If + past, would + V1 | If I were rich, I would travel. |
| Third | Impossible past | If + had + V3, would have + V3 | If I had studied, I would have passed. |

#### Common Errors:

**Error 1: Using "will" in if-clause**
❌ If it **will rain**, we will cancel.
✔ If it **rains**, we will cancel.

**Error 2: Using "would" in if-clause**
❌ If I **would** be rich, I would travel.
✔ If I **were** rich, I would travel.

**Error 3: Using "was" instead of "were" in Second Conditional**
❌ If I **was** you, I would accept.
✔ If I **were** you, I would accept.
❌ If she **was** here, she would help.
✔ If she **were** here, she would help.

#### 🔥 **CONDITIONAL SHORTCUT:**
- If-clause (condition) → NEVER use will/would/should/could
- Main clause (result) → use will/would/should/could

#### 📝 **CONDITIONAL CHEAT SHEET:**

| Situation | If-clause | Main clause |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| Possible future | Present tense | will + V1 |
| Unlikely present | Past tense | would + V1 |
| Impossible past | had + V3 | would have + V3 |

### TYPE 14: GERUND/INFINITIVE ERRORS

#### Verbs followed by GERUND (-ing)

| Verb | Example |
|------|---------|
| enjoy | I enjoy **playing** cricket. |
| avoid | Avoid **eating** junk food. |
| consider | Consider **applying** for the job. |
| suggest | She suggested **going** to the park. |
| mind | Do you mind **waiting**? |
| finish | He finished **writing** the letter. |
| practice | Practice **speaking** English. |
| admit | He admitted **stealing** the money. |
| deny | She denied **breaking** the vase. |
| imagine | Imagine **living** on the moon. |
| risk | Don't risk **losing** your job. |
| miss | I miss **seeing** you. |
| keep | Keep **trying**. |
| quit | He quit **smoking**. |

#### Verbs followed by INFINITIVE (to + V1)

| Verb | Example |
|------|---------|
| want | I want **to play** cricket. |
| decide | She decided **to apply** for the job. |
| hope | I hope **to see** you soon. |
| plan | They plan **to build** a house. |
| promise | He promised **to help** me. |
| agree | She agreed **to come**. |
| refuse | He refused **to pay**. |
| learn | I learned **to swim**. |

| remember | Remember **to lock** the door. |
| need | You need **to study**. |
| offer | He offered **to drive** me home. |
| seem | She seems **to be** happy. |

#### Verbs followed by BOTH (with meaning change)

| Verb | + Gerund | + Infinitive |
|------|----------|--------------|
| remember | I remember **locking** the door. (past memory) | Remember **to lock** the door. (don't forget) |
| forget | I'll never forget **meeting** her. (past memory) | Don't forget **to meet** her. (don't fail to) |
| stop | He stopped **smoking**. (quit) | He stopped **to smoke**. (paused for) |
| try | Try **pressing** the button. (experiment) | Try **to press** the button. (attempt) |
| regret | I regret **telling** him. (sorry for past) | I regret **to tell** you. (sorry to inform) |

### TYPE 15: QUESTION TAG ERRORS

#### Rule 1: Positive statement → Negative tag
| Statement | Tag |
|-----------|-----|
| She is coming, | **isn't she?** |
| You like coffee, | **don't you?** |
| He can swim, | **can't he?** |

#### Rule 2: Negative statement → Positive tag
| Statement | Tag |
|-----------|-----|
| She isn't coming, | **is she?** |
| You don't like coffee, | **do you?** |
| He can't swim, | **can he?** |

#### Rule 3: Auxiliary verb in statement → Same auxiliary in tag
| Statement | Tag |
|-----------|-----|
| She **is** coming, | **isn't** she? |
| They **have** arrived, | **haven't** they? |
| He **will** come, | **won't** he? |

#### Rule 4: No auxiliary → Use do/does/did
| Statement | Tag |
|-----------|-----|
| She **likes** coffee, | **doesn't** she? |
| They **came** yesterday, | **didn't** they? |
| You **eat** meat, | **don't** you? |

#### Rule 5: I am → aren't I? (not amn't I)
| Statement | Tag |
|-----------|-----|
| I am late, | **aren't I?** |

#### Rule 6: Let's → shall we?
| Statement | Tag |
|-----------|-----|
| Let's go, | **shall we?** |

#### Rule 7: Imperatives → will you? / won't you?
| Statement | Tag |
|-----------|-----|
| Open the door, | **will you?** |
| Don't be late, | **will you?** |

#### Rule 8: Nothing/Everything → Use "it"
| Statement | Tag |
|-----------|-----|
| Nothing is perfect, | **is it?** |
| Everything is fine, | **isn't it?** |

#### Rule 9: Nobody/Everyone → Use "they"
| Statement | Tag |
|-----------|-----|
| Nobody came, | **did they?** |
| Everyone is here, | **aren't they?** |

#### Rule 10: Never/No/Hardly/Scarcely → Positive tag
| Statement | Tag |
|-----------|-----|
| He never comes, | **does he?** |
| She hardly works, | **does she?** |

#### 🔥 **QUESTION TAG SHORTCUT:**
1. If statement is positive → tag is negative
2. If statement is negative → tag is positive
3. Use the auxiliary from the statement
4. If no auxiliary, use do/does/did
5. Pronoun in tag matches the subject

### TYPE 16: DIRECT-INDIRECT SPEECH (NARRATION)

#### 📌 **RULE 1: CHANGE OF TENSES**

| Direct Speech | Indirect Speech |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Simple Present | Simple Past |
| Present Continuous | Past Continuous |
| Present Perfect | Past Perfect |
| Simple Past | Past Perfect |
| Past Continuous | Past Perfect Continuous |
| Past Perfect | Past Perfect (no change) |
| Will | Would |
| Can | Could |
| May | Might |
| Shall | Should |
| Must | Had to / Must (no change if universal) |

**Examples:**
| Direct | Indirect |
|--------|----------|
| He said, "I **eat** apples." | He said that he **ate** apples. |
| She said, "I **am reading**." | She said that she **was reading**. |
| They said, "We **have finished**." | They said that they **had finished**. |
| He said, "I **saw** her." | He said that he **had seen** her. |
| She said, "I **will come**." | She said that she **would come**. |

#### ⚠️ **NO CHANGE IN TENSE IF:**

1. **Universal Truth:**
   - Direct: The teacher said, "The earth **is** round."
   - Indirect: The teacher said that the earth **is** round.

2. **Habitual Action:**
   - Direct: He said, "I **wake up** at 6 AM."
   - Indirect: He said that he **wakes up** at 6 AM.

3. **Reporting verb is in Present/Future:**
   - Direct: He **says**, "I **am** busy."
   - Indirect: He **says** that he **is** busy.

#### 📌 **RULE 2: CHANGE OF PRONOUNS**

| Direct | Indirect |
|--------|----------|
| I | (depends on subject of reporting verb) |
| We | (depends on subject) |
| You | (depends on object) |
| My | (depends on subject) |
| Our | (depends on subject) |
| Your | (depends on object) |

**Examples:**
- He said, "**I** am tired." → He said that **he** was tired.
- She said to me, "**You** are late." → She told me that **I** was late.
- They said, "**We** will help **you**." → They said that **they** would help **me**.

#### 📌 **RULE 3: CHANGE OF TIME & PLACE WORDS**

| Direct | Indirect |
|--------|----------|
| Now | Then |
| Today | That day |
| Yesterday | The previous day / The day before |
| Tomorrow | The next day / The following day |
| Here | There |
| This | That |
| These | Those |
| Ago | Before |
| Last week | The previous week |
| Next week | The following week |
| Tonight | That night |

**Examples:**
- He said, "I will come **tomorrow**." → He said that he would come **the next day**.
- She said, "I met him **yesterday**." → She said that she had met him **the previous day**.
- They said, "We are living **here**." → They said that they were living **there**.

#### 📌 **RULE 4: INTERROGATIVE SENTENCES**

**Yes/No Questions → Use if/whether**
| Direct | Indirect |
|--------|----------|
| He said to me, "**Do** you like coffee?" | He asked me **if** I liked coffee. |
| She said, "**Is** he coming?" | She asked **whether** he was coming. |

**WH-Questions → Use the WH-word**
| Direct | Indirect |
|--------|----------|
| He said to me, "**Where** do you live?" | He asked me **where** I lived. |
| She said, "**What** is your name?" | She asked **what** my name was. |

#### 📌 **RULE 5: IMPERATIVE SENTENCES**

| Direct | Indirect |
|--------|----------|
| "Sit down." | He ordered/told/asked to sit down. |
| "Don't smoke." | He forbade smoking. / He told not to smoke. |
| "Please help me." | He requested to help him. |

**Structure:** Subject + reporting verb (ordered/requested/advised) + to + V1

**Examples:**
- He said, "Open the door." → He told **to open** the door.
- She said, "Don't be late." → She warned **not to be** late.
- The teacher said, "Study hard." → The teacher advised **to study** hard.

#### 📌 **RULE 6: EXCLAMATORY SENTENCES**

| Direct | Indirect |
|--------|----------|
| "What a beautiful day!" | He exclaimed that it was a beautiful day. |
| "Hurray! We won!" | He exclaimed with joy that they had won. |
| "Alas! He is dead." | He exclaimed with sorrow that he was dead. |

**Reporting verbs:** exclaimed with joy/sorrow, cried out, applauded

### TYPE 17: ACTIVE-PASSIVE VOICE

#### 📊 **TENSE-WISE PASSIVE STRUCTURES**

| Tense | Active | Passive |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Simple Present | S + V1 + O | O + is/am/are + V3 + by + S |
| Present Continuous | S + is/am/are + V-ing + O | O + is/am/are + being + V3 + by + S |
| Present Perfect | S + has/have + V3 + O | O + has/have + been + V3 + by + S |
| Simple Past | S + V2 + O | O + was/were + V3 + by + S |
| Past Continuous | S + was/were + V-ing + O | O + was/were + being + V3 + by + S |
| Past Perfect | S + had + V3 + O | O + had + been + V3 + by + S |
| Simple Future | S + will + V1 + O | O + will + be + V3 + by + S |
| Future Perfect | S + will have + V3 + O | O + will have + been + V3 + by + S |
| Modals (can/may) | S + modal + V1 + O | O + modal + be + V3 + by + S |

#### 🔥 **PASSIVE VOICE SHORTCUT:**

**Formula:** Object + be verb + V3 + by + Subject

**Be verbs:**
- Present: is/am/are
- Past: was/were
- Perfect: been
- Continuous: being
- Future: be
- Modal + be

#### 📝 **EXAMPLES BY TENSE:**

**Simple Present:**
- Active: She **writes** a letter.
- Passive: A letter **is written** by her.

**Present Continuous:**
- Active: She **is writing** a letter.
- Passive: A letter **is being written** by her.

**Present Perfect:**
- Active: She **has written** a letter.
- Passive: A letter **has been written** by her.

**Simple Past:**
- Active: She **wrote** a letter.
- Passive: A letter **was written** by her.

**Past Continuous:**
- Active: She **was writing** a letter.
- Passive: A letter **was being written** by her.

**Past Perfect:**
- Active: She **had written** a letter.
- Passive: A letter **had been written** by her.

**Simple Future:**
- Active: She **will write** a letter.
- Passive: A letter **will be written** by her.

**Future Perfect:**
- Active: She **will have written** a letter.
- Passive: A letter **will have been written** by her.

**Modal Verbs:**
- Active: She **can write** a letter.
- Passive: A letter **can be written** by her.

#### 📌 **SPECIAL CASES:**

**Case 1: Two Objects**
- Active: She gave **me** **a book**.
- Passive 1: **I was given** a book by her. (more common)
- Passive 2: **A book was given** to me by her.

**Case 2: No Passive Possible**
- Intransitive verbs (no object): sleep, go, come, arrive, die
- ❌ The boy slept. → No passive

**Case 3: Prepositions remain after verb**
- Active: She **looked after** the child.
- Passive: The child **was looked after** by her.

**Case 4: Let + object + be + V3 (Imperative)**
- Active: Open the door.
- Passive: Let the door be opened.
- Active: Do not open the door.
- Passive: Let the door not be opened.

**Case 5: Verbs with two objects (give, send, offer, teach, promise)**
- Active: He taught **us** **English**.
- Passive 1: **We were taught** English by him.
- Passive 2: **English was taught** to us by him.

### TYPE 18: CLAUSES

#### 📌 **NOUN CLAUSE**

A clause that functions as a noun (subject, object, complement)

**Introduced by:** that, what, why, how, where, when, if, whether

**Examples:**
- **What he said** surprised me. (Subject)
- I know **that he is honest**. (Object)
- This is **what I wanted**. (Complement)

#### 📌 **ADJECTIVE CLAUSE (Relative Clause)**

A clause that modifies a noun/pronoun

**Introduced by:** who, whom, whose, which, that

**Examples:**
- The man **who came here** is my uncle.
- The book **that you gave me** is interesting.
- This is the house **where I was born**.

#### 📌 **ADVERB CLAUSE**

A clause that modifies a verb, adjective, or adverb

**Types:**

| Type | Conjunctions | Example |
|------|--------------|---------|
| Time | when, while, after, before, since | I will call you **when I arrive**. |
| Place | where, wherever | Sit **wherever you like**. |
| Reason | because, since, as | He failed **because he didn't study**. |
| Purpose | so that, in order that | Study hard **so that you can pass**. |
| Result | so...that, such...that | He was **so tired that he slept**. |
| Condition | if, unless, provided | **If it rains**, we will cancel. |
| Concession | though, although, even though | **Although he tried**, he failed. |
| Manner | as, as if, as though | Do **as I say**. |

### TYPE 24: SPELLING RULES AND COMMON MISSPELLINGS

#### 📌 **SPELLING RULES**

**Rule 1: I before E except after C**
- believe, chief, field, receive, ceiling, deceive
- **Exceptions:** weird, science, sufficient, ancient

**Rule 2: Silent E Rule**
- When adding -ing, drop the e: write → writing, dance → dancing
- When adding -ed, keep the e? No: hope → hoped (drop e), agree → agreed (keep if double e)

**Rule 3: Y to I Rule**
- Change Y to I when adding -es or -ed: cry → cries, study → studied
- Keep Y when adding -ing: cry → crying, study → studying

**Rule 4: Double the consonant when:**
- One syllable word ending in consonant-vowel-consonant: stop → stopped, big → bigger
- Stress on last syllable: begin → beginning, prefer → preferred

### TYPE 25: SENTENCE ARRANGEMENT (PARA JUMBLES)

#### 📌 **STRATEGY FOR PARA JUMBLES**

**Step 1: Find the opening sentence**
- Look for sentences that introduce a topic, person, or idea
- Avoid sentences with: he, she, it, they, this, that (these refer to something already mentioned)
- Avoid sentences starting with: But, However, Therefore, So (these are connectors)

**Step 2: Look for mandatory pairs**
- Find sentences that MUST go together
- Look for: cause-effect, question-answer, statement-example

**Step 3: Look for linking words**

| Type | Words |
|------|-------|
| Addition | Moreover, Furthermore, Besides, Also |
| Contrast | However, But, Nevertheless, On the other hand |
| Cause/Effect | Therefore, Thus, Consequently, As a result |
| Time | Then, After that, Later, Meanwhile |
| Example | For example, For instance, Such as |

**Step 4: Check pronoun references**
- If a sentence has "he", there must be a previous sentence mentioning a male person
- If a sentence has "this", there must be a previous sentence mentioning what "this" refers to

**Step 5: Check logical flow**
- Introduction → Details → Examples → Conclusion

#### 🔥 **PARA JUMBLE SHORTCUTS:**

1. **The "Noun-Pronoun" Trick:** If sentence B has "he/she/it/they" and sentence A has a noun, then A comes before B.

2. **The "Time" Trick:** Arrange sentences in chronological order (past → present → future).

3. **The "General to Specific" Trick:** General statements come first, specific details come later.

4. **The "Question-Answer" Trick:** Question sentence comes first, answer sentence comes after.

5. **The "Cause-Effect" Trick:** Cause comes first, effect comes after.

#### 📝 **EXAMPLE PARA JUMBLE:**

**Arrange the following sentences in correct order:**

A. Therefore, it is important to manage time effectively.
B. Time management is a crucial skill for students.
C. For example, creating a study schedule can help.
D. Without proper time management, students often feel stressed.

**Solution:**
1. **B** - Introduces the topic (Time management is crucial)
2. **D** - Explains the problem (Without it, students feel stressed)
3. **A** - Gives the solution (Therefore, manage time)
4. **C** - Provides example (For example, study schedule)

**Correct order: B D A C**

### TYPE 26: FILL IN THE BLANKS

#### 📌 **STRATEGY FOR FILL IN THE BLANKS**

**Step 1: Read the entire sentence** to understand context

**Step 2: Identify what type of word is missing**
- Noun, verb, adjective, adverb, preposition, conjunction?

**Step 3: Check grammar around the blank**
- Before blank: article (a/an/the) → noun likely
- After blank: noun → adjective likely
- Subject + blank + object → verb likely

**Step 4: Check for fixed phrases/collocations**
- "He insisted ___ going" → on (fixed preposition)
- "She is capable ___ doing" → of (fixed preposition)

**Step 5: Check meaning/context**
- Positive or negative context?
- Formal or informal?

#### 🔥 **FILL IN THE BLANKS SHORTCUTS:**

1. **Preposition questions:** Look for fixed verb-preposition combinations (insist on, depend on, afraid of)

2. **Article questions:** Check the sound of the next word (a university, an hour)

3. **Conjunction questions:** Check relationship between clauses (cause-effect, contrast, addition)

4. **Tense questions:** Look for time markers (yesterday → past, since → perfect)

### TYPE 27: CLOZE TEST

#### 📌 **WHAT IS A CLOZE TEST?**
A passage with blanks where you must choose the correct word from options. Tests vocabulary, grammar, and comprehension together.

#### 📌 **STRATEGY FOR CLOZE TEST**

**Step 1: Skim the passage** quickly to understand the topic

**Step 2: Read the first sentence carefully** - it often sets the theme

**Step 3: For each blank, use context:**
- What part of speech is needed?
- What is the meaning required?
- What words commonly go together (collocations)?

**Step 4: Look for clues in surrounding sentences**

**Step 5: Use elimination** - remove options that don't fit grammatically or meaning-wise

#### 🔥 **CLOZE TEST SHORTCUTS:**

1. **Grammar clues:** If blank follows "the", it's likely a noun. If follows "is", it's adjective or -ing.

2. **Context clues:** The sentences before and after often contain the answer.

3. **Collocation clues:** Some words naturally go together (heavy rain, strong wind, make a decision).

4. **Repetition clue:** The same word might appear nearby.

## 🎯 **ARTICLES - QUICK CHEAT SHEET**

| Use 'A' Before | Use 'AN' Before |
|----------------|-----------------|
| Consonant SOUND | Vowel SOUND |
| a **university** (yu) | an **hour** (ow) |
| a **European** (yu) | an **honest** (o) |
| a **one-eyed** (wu) | an **MBA** (em) |
| a **useful** (yu) | an **FBI** (ef) |
| a **house** (h) | an **apple** (a) |

| Use 'THE' | Don't Use 'THE' |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Unique things (sun, moon) | Proper nouns (Mumbai) |
| Superlatives (best) | Abstract nouns (life) |
| Ordinals (first) | Plural general (dogs) |
| Musical instruments (piano) | Meals (breakfast) |
| Rivers, seas (Ganges) | Languages (Hindi) |
| Mountain ranges (Himalayas) | Sports (cricket) |
| Plural countries (USA) | Most countries (India) |

## 🎯 **PRONOUNS - QUICK CHEAT SHEET**

| Subject | Object | Possessive | Reflexive |
|---------|--------|------------|-----------|
| I | me | my/mine | myself |
| You | you | your/yours | yourself |
| He | him | his | himself |
| She | her | her/hers | herself |
| It | it | its | itself |
| We | us | our/ours | ourselves |
| They | them | their/theirs | themselves |

**Who vs Whom:**
- Who = HE/SHE/THEY (subject)
- Whom = HIM/HER/THEM (object)

## 🎯 **CONDITIONALS - QUICK CHEAT SHEET**

|------|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Zero | Present | Present | If you heat ice, it melts. |

## ⚡ **TOP 10 EXAM TIPS**

1. **Read the complete sentence first** before looking at options
2. **Trust your ear** for 80% of questions - if it sounds wrong, it probably is
3. **Look for time markers** (yesterday, since, for, ago) - they dictate tense
4. **Check subject-verb agreement** first - most common error
5. **Watch for double comparatives** (more better, most tallest)
6. **Memorize fixed prepositions** - they repeat frequently
7. **Know the a/an sound rule** - it's about sound, not letter
8. **Eliminate two wrong options first** then choose between remaining
9. **Don't spend more than 1 minute** on any question
10. **If completely stuck, mark for review** and move on

## ⚡ **COMMON TRICK QUESTIONS TO WATCH FOR**

1. **Trick:** "One of the boys are..." → Should be "is"
2. **Trick:** "She don't like..." → Should be "doesn't"
3. **Trick:** "Between you and I..." → Should be "me"
4. **Trick:** "I have seen him yesterday..." → Should be "saw"
5. **Trick:** "He is an university..." → Should be "a university"
6. **Trick:** "Return back..." → Just "return"
7. **Trick:** "More smarter..." → Just "smarter"
8. **Trick:** "If I was you..." → Should be "were"
9. **Trick:** "The news are..." → Should be "is"
10. **Trick:** "Scissors is..." → Should be "are"
